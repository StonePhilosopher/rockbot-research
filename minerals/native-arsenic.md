# Mineral Species Research — Vugg Simulator

## Species: Native Arsenic

### Identity
- **Formula:** As (elemental arsenic)
- **Crystal system:** Trigonal (rhombohedral; space group R-3m, layered structure analogous to graphite but with buckled sheets)
- **Mineral group:** Native element (metalloid)
- **Hardness (Mohs):** 3.5
- **Specific gravity:** 5.63–5.78
- **Cleavage:** Basal {0001} perfect (on the layered sheets)
- **Fracture:** Uneven to subconchoidal
- **Luster:** Metallic, tarnishes dull

### Color & Appearance
- **Typical color:** Tin-white to steel-gray on fresh surfaces; tarnishes rapidly to dark gray or black with slight iridescence
- **Color causes:** Metallic bonding; oxidation of surface to arsenolite (As₂O₃) causes darkening
- **Transparency:** Opaque
- **Streak:** Black
- **Notable visual features:** Layered, foliated structure; thin sheets peel apart like graphite or mica. Often massive or granular rather than crystalline. Bright metallic luster when freshly broken but tarnishes within hours.

### Crystal Habits
- **Primary habit:** Granular, massive, reniform (kidney-shaped), botryoidal; rarely as small rhombohedral crystals
- **Common forms/faces:** {0001} basal plates; rare hexagonal prisms
- **Twin laws:** None reported
- **Varieties:** None established
- **Special morphologies:** Layered or foliated masses; earthy coatings on other arsenic-bearing minerals

### Formation Conditions (SIMULATOR PARAMETERS)

#### Temperature
- **Nucleation temperature range:** 100–400°C (hypogene hydrothermal)
- **Optimal growth temperature:** 150–300°C
- **Decomposition temperature:** Sublimes at 615°C (direct solid-to-gas transition, no liquid); melts under pressure at 817°C (atmospheric pressure sublimation preferred)
- **Temperature-dependent habits:** Higher T → coarser granular masses; lower T → fine earthy aggregates and coatings

#### Chemistry Required
- **Required elements in broth:** As (high concentration, >50 ppm)
- **Optional/enhancing elements:** Sb, Bi, Co, Ni, Fe, Ag (these form sulfides/arsenides that compete with native As for the As budget)
- **Inhibiting elements:** S (forms arsenopyrite FeAsS, loellingite FeAs₂, cobaltite CoAsS, niccolite NiAs — all consume As before native As can form); Cu (forms tennantite Cu₁₂As₄S₁₃); O₂ at high levels (oxidizes As to arsenate)
- **Required pH range:** 4–8 (acidic to mildly alkaline)
- **Required Eh range:** Strongly reducing (Eh -300 to -100 mV) — arsenic must remain as As⁰ or As³⁻, not oxidize to As⁵⁺
- **Required O₂ range:** Very low — arsenic oxidizes readily even in mildly oxidizing conditions

#### Secondary Chemistry Release
- **Does it release any chemicals when forming?** No direct release; As⁰ precipitation from fluid consumes dissolved arsenite (H₃AsO₃) or arsenate (H₃AsO₄) reduced to elemental form
- **Byproducts of nucleation:** Minor H⁺ release if reduction proceeds via aqueous pathways
- **Byproducts of dissolution/decomposition:** Oxidation → arsenolite (As₂O₃) and claudetite (As₂O₃ polymorph); further oxidation → arsenates (scorodite FeAsO₄·2H₂O, pharmacolite CaHAsO₄·2H₂O)

#### Growth Characteristics
- **Relative growth rate:** Slow to moderate
- **Maximum crystal size:** Rarely >1 cm; usually mm-scale or smaller
- **Typical crystal size in vugs:** Sub-mm to 5 mm
- **Does growth rate change with temperature?** Growth faster at higher T but vapor loss (sublimation) also accelerates
- **Competes with:** All arsenide and sulfarsenide minerals — arsenopyrite, loellingite, cobaltite, nickeline, tennantite, maucherite (Ni₁₁As₈), safflorite (CoAs₂), skutterudite (CoAs₃), rammelsbergite (NiAs₂)

#### Stability
- **Breaks down in heat?** Sublimes at 615°C (direct solid→gas); melts at 817°C only under pressure
- **Breaks down in light?** No
- **Dissolves in water?** Insoluble; slightly soluble in alkaline solutions forming arsenites
- **Dissolves in acid?** Soluble in hot concentrated HNO₃ and aqua regia; insoluble in HCl; attacked by alkali sulfides
- **Oxidizes?** Rapidly — tarnishes in air to arsenolite (As₂O₃); full oxidation in weathering zones produces scorodite, pharmacolite, and other arsenates
- **Dehydrates?** N/A
- **Radiation sensitivity:** None notable

### Paragenesis
- **Forms AFTER:** Arsenopyrite, loellingite, cobaltite, nickeline — native As is the residual phase when As exceeds the stoichiometric demand of all arsenides
- **Forms BEFORE:** Oxidation-zone arsenates (scorodite, pharmacolite, erythrite, annabergite) — native As oxidizes upward into the supergene profile
- **Commonly associated minerals:** Arsenopyrite (FeAsS), loellingite (FeAs₂), cobaltite (CoAsS), nickeline (NiAs), realgar (AsS), orpiment (As₂S₃), pyrite, marcasite, native silver, native bismuth, stibnite, galena, sphalerite, chalcopyrite
- **Zone:** Hypogene (primary hydrothermal) to transitional (just above the sulfide zone, below the oxidation front)
- **Geological environment:** Medium-temperature hydrothermal veins, Co-Ni-Ag-Bi-As vein systems, contact metasomatic skarns with arsenic enrichment, epithermal systems with arsenic overprint

### Famous Localities
- **Classic locality 1:** Andreasberg, Harz Mountains, Germany — historic type locality for native arsenic; fine reniform and botryoidal masses in silver-cobalt veins
- **Classic locality 2:** Schneeberg, Saxony, Germany — classic European source; layered foliated masses with native silver, bismuth, and cobaltite
- **Classic locality 3:** Cobalt, Ontario, Canada — silver-cobalt arsenic five-element vein system; native As with silver, cobaltite, niccolite, skutterudite
- **Notable specimens:** Keweenaw Peninsula, Michigan (native As with native Cu in amygdaloidal basalt); Trepča, Kosovo (five-element veins); Bou Azzer, Morocco (Co-As deposits)

### Fluorescence
- **Fluorescent under UV?** No
- **SW (255nm) color:** None
- **MW (310nm) color:** None
- **LW (365nm) color:** None
- **Phosphorescent?** No

### Flavor Text

> Arsenic is the poison that chose to be a mineral. In its native form it is a soft, tin-white metalloid that peels apart in thin folia like graphite — but unlike graphite, the sheets are deadly. It forms only in hydrothermal systems so rich in arsenic that every sulfide and arsenide has already eaten its fill: arsenopyrite, loellingite, cobaltite, nickeline, realgar, orpiment — the entire arsenic zoo must be sated before native arsenic crystallizes as the leftover. And even then it barely survives. In air it tarnishes to arsenolite within hours, its surface fogging from bright metal to dull oxide. In the weathering zone it oxidizes completely to scorodite and pharmacolite, spreading its toxicity through the groundwater. The mineral is a paradox: a native element that is never truly at rest, chemically restless even at Earth's surface, doomed to dissolve into the very poison that defines it. Collectors handle it carefully. The finest specimens come from the silver-cobalt veins of Andreasberg and Cobalt — places where arsenic accumulated in quantities that almost killed the mines that mined it.

### Simulator Implementation Notes
- **New parameters needed:** trace_As already tracked; no new parameters needed
- **New events needed:** `event_arsenic_reduction` — a localized strongly reducing pulse (Eh drops sharply, pH shifts acidic) that can precipitate native As from arsenite/arsenate-rich fluids. Rare event, analogous to gold telluride pulses but for As systems.
- **Nucleation rule pseudocode:**
```
IF trace_As > 50 AND Eh < -100 mV AND pH 4-8
   AND all arsenide-forming metals (Fe, Co, Ni, Cu) are depleted OR already bound in arsenides/arsenopyrites
   AND T between 100-400°C → nucleate native_arsenic
rate = slow to moderate
priority = low (arsenides form first)
```
- **Growth rule pseudocode:**
```
IF trace_As > 20 AND Eh < -50 mV AND T in (100, 400) → grow at rate 0.3 (moderate)
habit: if T > 250 → granular/massive; if T < 200 → foliated/earthy coatings
```
- **Habit selection logic:** Higher T → coarser granular/reniform masses; lower T → fine earthy coatings on fracture walls; foliated habit when space permits layer-by-layer growth
- **Decomposition products:** Oxidation → arsenolite (As₂O₃) → scorodite (FeAsO₄·2H₂O); sublimation at >615°C → gaseous As (highly toxic, simulated as As loss to fluid)

### Variants for Game
- **Variant 1: Foliated arsenic** — Layered, foliated masses peeling apart like graphite or mica. Lower T, slow growth. Classic Andreasberg type.
- **Variant 2: Granular/reniform arsenic** — Coarse kidney-shaped botryoidal masses. Higher T, moderate growth. Cobalt, Ontario type.
- **Variant 3: Oxidized arsenic** — Surface coated with arsenolite (white powdery crust) or scorodite (yellow-green crust). Cosmetic variant triggered by brief O₂ spike after formation. Represents weathering transition.

## Linked Paragenetic Sequence: The Arsenic System

Native arsenic does not form in isolation. It occupies the **residual position** in a well-defined arsenic paragenetic sequence that spans the hypogene zone. Understanding the full sequence is essential for simulator implementation.

### Sequence: Early → Late

1. **Arsenopyrite (FeAsS)** — Early, high-T (~400–550°C). The first and most abundant arsenic sink. Fe + As + S combine before native As can form.
2. **Loellingite (FeAs₂)** — Early, high-T (~350–500°C). Iron-arsenide, forms where S is scarce relative to As.
3. **Cobaltite (CoAsS)** — Early-mid, moderate T (~300–500°C). Co + As + S.
4. **Nickeline (NiAs)** — Mid, moderate T (~250–450°C). Ni + As.
5. **Skutterudite (CoAs₃)** / **Safflorite (CoAs₂)** / **Rammelsbergite (NiAs₂)** — Mid, various T. Metal:As ratios vary.
6. **Realgar (AsS)** — Mid-low T (~100–300°C). As + S in equal proportions.
7. **Orpiment (As₂S₃)** — Low T (~100–250°C). As-rich sulfide.
8. **NATIVE ARSENIC (As)** — Late, residual. Only forms when arsenide/sulfide sinks are exhausted and excess As remains in reducing fluid.

### Oxidation Zone Transformation
Above the water table, the entire sequence oxidizes:
- **Native As → arsenolite (As₂O₃) → scorodite (FeAsO₄·2H₂O)**
- **Arsenopyrite → scorodite + beudantite + jarosite**
- **Realgar → arsenolite / scorodite** (plus pararealgar intermediate)
- **Orpiment → arsenolite / scorodite**

### Simulator Events
- `event_arsenic_pulse` — early-stage As-rich fluid injection (hypogene)
- `event_arsenic_reduction` — late-stage strongly reducing pulse that can precipitate native As (rare)
- `event_oxidation_front` — triggers oxidation of native As and all arsenides to arsenates
